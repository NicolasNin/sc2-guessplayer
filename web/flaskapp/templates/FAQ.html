What is SC2guess ?

Sc2guess is a project which aims at finding the players in a starcraft 2 replay. It uses databases of replays such as the WCS where the names in the replay are usually correct.

What it's not ?

It's not another sc2unmasked who are already doing a good job at finding names behind GM barcodes, BUT it could help them .

How does it works ?

It assumes that a player (at least a good player), have regular patterns in keyboard use. In particular it looks at the hotkeys a player uses and compares it to the one in the database

How do I use it ?

  • Upload a replay where you don't know the players or are not sure of who are playing
  • For the two players in the replay three guesses are given with an "overall proximity score"
  • The highest the score the better, a score above 90% is very likely
  • If no score seems good, either the player is not in the database, or the algorithm doesn't detect it

How reliable is it ?

The percentage given is kind of a bullshit number derived from a distance between the two replays, and doesnt directly represents the probability of a player being a good one . This number was meant to have few false-positive, which means that an above 90% score is very good. And also that sometimes a 1% response can be good.

For example if we use the wcs 2015 season 2 as a database reference and test the wcs 2015 season 3, we have :

  • 1070 (half)-replays where the player belongs to the two seasons
  • Among those we have 38 failures
  • Among those 38 failures we have 16 where the good guesses is really far (ie not a second or third guess)
  • On this particular example there is a 96% success rate
In general if the two databases are not too far apart in time the success rate is around 90% or more.

Where are the replays in the database from ?

The replay are from official tournaments, where players usually use their true name and not barcodes:
  • The WCS 2015 three seasons.
  • The nation wars 3 (a few barcodes there)
  • The home story cup 3
  • The Dreamhack open Leipzig

What does all those colored bars means ?

The results are given by calculating some features of the players.

  • The hotkeys score, is a distance between the hotkey use by the two players, a rule of thumb is that 0.3 or less is pretty good
  • The "gap" score is a measure of the speed ot the player (more precisely a frequency of very short time timeframe between hotkey strokes). A 0.4 score is good.
  • Finally the APM is not really useful and not really the APM. It should be corelated to the previous items and is not used to score the players
Among the three the hotkey score is by far the most meaningful. The "gap" score was getting more good results in testings by being able to disinguish between two players with close hotkey scores ( Zanster and Life for exemple). As a rule there is less variation in gap among pros than in hotkeys.

I've tested a player who is in the database and it doesn't match ?

It's possible that the replay you tested and the one from the player in the database are too far apart. It seems that different expansions change somethings. Another possibility is that the player has changed enough of his hotkeys pattern to be effectively another player (see the Zest case) (apart from the gap). Finally some player can have some variance in the way they use hotkeys and if they are closed to a few others players, those can match first.
